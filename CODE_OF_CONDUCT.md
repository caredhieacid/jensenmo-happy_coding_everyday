# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

We want a technical community where people can challenge ideas directly without diminishing the
people presenting them. Contributors, maintainers, and users should:

- discuss observable behavior and evidence;
- make scope, uncertainty, and trade-offs explicit;
- assume good intent while remaining precise about impact;
- welcome corrections from different languages, backgrounds, and levels of experience;
- protect private code, credentials, personal data, and security reports.

## Unacceptable behavior

Harassment, discrimination, threats, doxxing, sexualized conduct, sustained personal attacks,
credential exposure, or knowingly publishing private source and user data are not acceptable.
Disagreement about a design is welcome; intimidation and contempt are not.

## Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through an appropriate private GitHub
channel. Reports will be reviewed for context, impact, and conflicts of interest. Responses may range
from a clarification or warning to removal of content, temporary participation limits, or a ban.

Project maintainers are responsible for applying this standard in repository-managed spaces and for
protecting the privacy of reporters as far as practical.
